# DP Tour and Travels — Product Requirements Document

## 1. Project Overview

DP Tour and Travels operates a car-leasing model connecting two groups of external users through the company: **Clients**, who supply brand-new cars to the company, and **Dealers**, who receive those cars for operational use. The company sits in the middle, managing the financial relationship, legal agreements, documentation, and scheduling between both sides.

This document consolidates the requirements discussed in the client meeting into a structured set of product deliverables covering: user roles & permissions, the payment lifecycle, notifications, agreement generation, the public website, and the Client and Dealer portals.

### 1.1 Core Positioning (for Landing Page copy)

DP Tour and Travels builds relationships, not just deals — the company frames itself as connecting car owners and the business like family, backed by full satisfaction and approval of the car owner at every step.

### 1.2 Key Business Rules

- Only brand-new cars are accepted, taken on for a fixed term of 5 years.
- During the 5-year term, the car owner (client) is only responsible for the car's insurance.
- The company handles maintenance, servicing, driver, fuel, challans, and all other car-related issues.
- Once handed over, the car becomes the company's full responsibility.
- A slot must be booked on the portal before physical delivery of the car — cars delivered without a booked slot will not be accepted.
- The first month's payment is retained by the company as a security deposit.
- The client's first payout is released after 2 months + 10 days from car handover.

## 2. User Roles & Permissions

The platform supports four roles. Superadmin sits above all others with full RBAC (role-based access control) authority.

| Role | Summary | Permissions / Responsibilities |
|---|---|---|
| Superadmin | Full system control | Create/manage all Admin & Finance accounts (RBAC); bulk-create accounts via CSV upload with auto-generated passwords; holds all Finance permissions; manually closes fulfilled car requirements; reviews and contacts dealer applicants. |
| Finance | Payments & scheduling | Records the car receipt date from client; edits client payout amount and dealer collection amount; processes all payments made to clients and collected from dealers; generates and confirms Client & Dealer agreements; receives 7-day / 3-day / due-date payment reminders. |
| Client | Car owner | Views registered car(s), registration date, payment type (cash/cheque/online), and payment status; uploads required documents; views/downloads agreement; resets own password. |
| Dealer | Car recipient | Views available/upcoming stock; requests cars from stock; views full details of assigned car(s) and first-party (client) details; views/downloads own agreement; resets own password. |

### 2.1 Account Provisioning

- Superadmin can create new Admin/Finance accounts individually, or in bulk via CSV upload.
- Passwords for bulk-created accounts are auto-generated by the system.
- Clients and Dealers have a self-service "Forgot/Reset Password" option.
- Superadmin can access, edit, or reassign permissions for any user or Finance account (RBAC — define exactly which actions each admin type can perform).

## 3. Car & Payment Lifecycle

This is the core financial workflow of the platform, tracking a car from receipt through to client payout.

### 3.1 Workflow Steps

1. Client hands over their car to the company. Finance logs the car receipt date (e.g., 1 July 2026) in the system.
2. On submission, the system automatically calculates the payout due date as Receipt Date + 70 days (e.g., 1 July 2026 → 10 September 2026).
3. The client's account receives a notification confirming their car's payment has been scheduled for the calculated date.
4. The payout amount (e.g., ₹30,000) is configurable per car/client and can be edited only by Finance or Superadmin.
5. On the due date, if the client has collected the payment, Finance (or the system) marks it as paid; the record shows GREEN.
6. If the client has not collected payment by the due date, the record shows RED, flagging it as outstanding.
7. If the client returns later to collect an outstanding (RED) payment, the system automatically updates the record to reflect that payment has now been collected.

### 3.2 Status Indicators

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| GREEN | Payment to the client was completed on/around the scheduled due date. |
| RED | Payment was NOT collected by the client on the scheduled due date; flagged as outstanding. |

### 3.3 Editable by Finance

- Amount payable to the client (car EMI/payout value).
- Amount collectable from the dealer.

> Note: pricing edits should be logged (who changed what, and when) for audit purposes — recommended even though not explicitly requested, to protect both company and client in disputes.

## 4. Notification System

Finance accounts receive automated reminders ahead of each scheduled payment/collection date, on the following schedule:

- 7 days before the due date
- 3 days before the due date
- On the due date itself

Reminders are generated for both directions of cash flow:

- Payments the company owes to a Client.
- Payments the company is due to collect from a Dealer.

Clients additionally receive a one-time notification when their payout is first scheduled (see Section 3.1), and again when their agreement is confirmed and issued (see Section 5).

## 5. Agreement Generation Module

Both Client and Dealer agreements are produced from the same auto-generation engine, using a fixed, pre-approved agreement layout — the user only fills in variable fields.

### 5.1 Agreement Fields

- Car name / model
- Rate (payout amount)
- Owner name (client name)
- Car registration number
- Aadhaar UID

### 5.2 Agreement Flow

1. Finance enters the required details into the agreement form.
2. The system auto-generates the agreement from the fixed template and renders a preview within the website.
3. The agreement can be downloaded as a PDF at this preview stage.
4. Clicking "Confirm Agreement" finalizes it and triggers a notification to the client that their agreement has been issued.

The same generation flow (template, fields, preview, confirm, PDF download, notification) is reused for Dealer agreements.

## 6. Public Website

### 6.1 Landing Page

Contains the company positioning and value proposition copy (Section 1.1–1.2), plus two dedicated sections detailed below.

#### 6.1.1 Terms & Conditions (displayed on landing page)

1. Slot pre-booking is mandatory before delivering a car.
2. Only brand-new cars are accepted.
3. 5-year insurance must be provided by the car owner.
4. Only white-plate (private) cars are accepted.
5. GPS devices in the car are strictly prohibited.
6. The car owner must be handing over their car voluntarily.
7. No installment may be missed during the 5-year term — missing a payment results in the owner being blacklisted and barred from running a car with DP Tour and Travels.
8. Cars with more than 1,500–2,000 km on the odometer will not be accepted.

Additional note to display near the T&Cs: clients should double-check the exact variant of the car they intend to hand over and purchase that same variant from the showroom, as agreed at survey stage.

#### 6.1.2 Vehicle Requirement Catalogue

A public table listing the business's current car requirements, so prospective clients can see live demand and book a slot directly.

| S. No. | Vehicle Model & Color | Qty. Required | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Maruti Suzuki Brezza — White | 50 | [ Book Slot ] |
| 2 | Maruti Suzuki Brezza — Silver | 20 | [ Book Slot ] |
| 3 | Hyundai Creta — White | 30 | [ Book Slot ] |

"Book Slot" routes into the car-requirement application flow described in Section 6.3.

### 6.2 "Apply for Dealer" Page

When a visitor clicks "Apply for Dealer," the page first displays the eligibility requirements needed to become a dealer, before any application form is shown.

### 6.3 Car Requirements / Slot Booking Flow

This is the intake mechanism for new client supply, driven by live business demand.

1. The business posts its current car requirements (e.g., "need 50 Brezza cars") into the Car Requirements tab, shown in tabular format (Section 6.1.2).
2. A visitor who has matching cars clicks into a requirement row and enters: number of cars they have, name, phone number, and car name.
3. The Terms & Conditions (Section 6.1.1) are shown and must be accepted.
4. On acceptance, a client account is auto-created and the submitted car count/details are saved to the database.
5. The application appears on the Superadmin dashboard, where the Superadmin can review and contact the applicant.
6. Once a posted requirement is fully satisfied, the Superadmin manually closes it; it is then removed from the public-facing requirements list.

## 7. Client Portal

### 7.1 Access

- Login via User ID and Password.
- Self-service password reset.

### 7.2 Dashboard

After login, the client sees all cars they have registered with the company, e.g.:

| Car Model | Registration No. | Registration Date |
|---|---|---|
| Maruti Brezza | DL9-8-CDD-9 | 9-Jan-2026 |

### 7.3 Payment Visibility

- Scheduled payout date (receipt date + 70 days).
- Payment status — GREEN (paid) / RED (outstanding), per Section 3.2.
- Payment method: cheque, cash, or online.

### 7.4 Document Upload

Clients must complete the following document checklist before onboarding is considered finished:

| # | Document | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Aadhaar Card | Identity proof |
| 2 | PAN Card | Identity / tax proof |
| 3 | Driving Licence | Identity / eligibility proof |
| 4 | Passport-size Photograph | Client profile record |
| 5 | Car RC (Registration Certificate) | Must be issued within the last 2 months |
| 6 | Car Number Plate Photo | Photo of the plate, taken with the car |
| 7 | Signed Agreement | Auto-generated agreement, uploaded/stored post-confirmation |

### 7.5 Agreement Access

- View the confirmed agreement inside the portal.
- Download the agreement as a PDF.

## 8. Dealer Portal

### 8.1 Access

- Login via User ID and Password, with self-service password reset.

### 8.2 Stock & Requests

1. After login, the dealer can view full current stock and upcoming stock of available cars.
2. The dealer selects the cars/quantities they want; the request is saved to the database as a demand record for that dealer.

### 8.3 Assigned Car Details

Once a car is assigned to a dealer, the dealer can view its full details, including:

- Lease start date and end date.
- First-party (client/owner) details.

### 8.4 Obligations

- Returning the car on time is mandatory.
- Dealers must follow all company rules and system processes.

### 8.5 Dealer Agreement

Generated through the same Agreement Generation Module described in Section 5 (fixed template, field entry, preview, PDF download, confirm & notify).

## 9. Cross-Cutting Requirements Summary

- RBAC: Superadmin can view/edit any user or Finance account and configure what each admin type is permitted to do.
- Audit trail recommended on all price edits, agreement confirmations, and status changes (GREEN/RED).
- CSV bulk-import for account creation with auto-generated credentials.
- All notifications (client payout scheduled, agreement issued, Finance 7/3/0-day reminders) should be logged and viewable in-app, not just sent as one-off alerts.
- PDF export required for both Client and Dealer agreements.
- Mobile-responsive front end for the public landing page, given dealer applicants and clients are likely to browse from mobile.
